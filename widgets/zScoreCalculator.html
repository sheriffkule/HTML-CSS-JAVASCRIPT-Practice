About Z-Scores

A Z-Score (or standard score) represents how many standard deviations an element is from the mean. It's a way to compare results from different normal distributions.

Formula: Z = (X - μ) / σ

  • Z = Z-Score
  • X = Raw score
  • μ = Mean of the population
  • σ = Standard deviation of the population
